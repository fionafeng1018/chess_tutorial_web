The following are the most basic rules in Go:

  • Black makes the first move.
  • Once the stone is placed, should not be allowed to shift to another point.
  • A player may pass their turn at any time.
  • The stone must be placed on the intersection but not in the square.

  • The stone is not allowed to be placed on the “forbidden point”, which means the stone has no liberty when being placed on that point, unless this move can take away the opponent’s stone.

  • To pick up the opponent’s stone in a ko (“see-saw fight”) situation, the player should make another move instead of capturing the stone immediately.

  • Any stones that have no liberties after being captured should be taken away from the board.

  • The winner is determined by the number of stones that player has on the board plus the points in empty area surrounded by that player's stones. (1 stone = 1 point) Black needs 185 points to win the game, and the white needs 177 points.
